Spatial Pattern Analysis

Discussion

This maps illustrate the temporal changes in Chennai City's urban spatial patterns from 1990 to 2020, classifying areas into "Scattered Settlement," "Sub Urban Fringe," "Secondary Core," and "Primary Core." Over this 30-year period, there is a clear and dramatic increase in the extent of urbanized areas, particularly the "Primary Core" and "Secondary Core." In 1990, the urban core was relatively compact, primarily concentrated along the coast. By 2000, both the "Primary" and "Secondary Cores" show expansion, extending further inland and along the coastline. This trend accelerates significantly in 2010, with the "Primary Core" becoming much more widespread and contiguous, integrating several "Secondary Core" areas. By 2020, the maps depict a highly urbanized Chennai, where the "Primary Core" and "Secondary Core" areas dominate a substantial portion of the city, indicating widespread urban sprawl and the densification of previously less developed regions. This visual progression highlights the rapid and extensive urbanization that Chennai has undergone.
